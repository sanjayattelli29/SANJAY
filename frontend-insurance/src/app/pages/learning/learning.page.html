01. The Fundamentals

🎯

What is Insurance?

At its core, insurance is a financial safety net. It's a contract (the policy) where you pay a small amount (premium) to an insurance company, which in return agrees to pay for your losses if something unexpected happens.

📜

The Policy Document

This is your legal contract. It outlines what is covered (inclusions), what isn't (exclusions), and the terms you must follow to remain protected. Always read the fine print!

💰

Premium vs. Deductible

The Premium is the price you pay to keep the policy active. The Deductible is the amount you pay out of pocket before the insurance company pays the rest of a claim.

🛡️

Inclusions & Exclusions

Inclusions are events covered by your policy. Exclusions are specific situations where the insurer won't pay. Knowing both ensures no surprises during a claim.

⏳

Grace Period

A Grace Period is extra time (usually 15-30 days) to pay your premium after the due date. Your coverage stays active during this window, preventing policy lapse.

👥

Nominees & Beneficiaries

A Nominee is the person you choose to receive the insurance benefits in your absence. Keeping nominee details updated ensures quick support to your loved ones.

📈

Claim Settlement Ratio (CSR)

CSR is the percentage of claims a company pays out of total claims received. A high CSR indicates the insurer's reliability and commitment to its promises.

⚖️

Principle of Indemnity

The goal of insurance is to return you to the same financial position you were in before the loss—not to provide a profit. It's about "making you whole" again.

03. The Claims Journey

📢

1. Intimation

Notify the insurer as soon as an incident occurs. Modern apps like AcciSure allow for instant digital intimation.

🔍

2. Investigation

A Claims Officer reviews the details and may send a surveyor to assess the damage or verify the incident details.

✅

3. Adjudication

The Adjudicator decides if the claim falls within policy terms and determines the final payout amount.

📁

4. Verification

Checking the validity of documents like medical bills, police reports (FIR), and proof of incident to prevent fraud.

📏

5. Survey

A professional surveyor evaluates the extent of loss or damage on-site to provide an unbiased assessment report.

⚖️

6. Admissibility

The insurer confirms if the loss is covered under the specific terms and conditions of your active policy.

🏦

7. Approval

Final internal review and sign-off on the settlement amount calculated during the adjudication process.

💸

8. Disbursement

The approved funds are transferred directly to your bank account or to the hospital if it's a cashless claim.

04. The Ecosystem

Insurance Agent
The primary contact who helps you select the right policy for your needs.
Underwriter
Evaluates the risk of insuring you and decides the premium terms.
Claims Officer
Manages the claim process from start to finish once an incident is reported.
Adjudicator
Professional who makes the final decision on claim approval and settlement.
Actuary
Mathematical expert who calculates risks and premiums for the entire company.
